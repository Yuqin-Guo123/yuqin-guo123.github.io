A 3D scanning project on packaged masculinity in dating culture

This project explores how male personas are constructed within contemporary dating culture and visual media. Using four male models as subjects, I applied 3D scanning to transform them into toy-like figures presented inside Barbie-style packaging. By selectively amplifying familiar behavioral traits and socially recognizable stereotypes as a visual language, the project reflects on how certain male identity types are shaped, repeated, and circulated in contemporary culture. The final outcome takes the form of a commercial-style video that examines how identity can be packaged, performed, and mediated through advertising aesthetics. The animated background sequences in the video were generated through a vibe coding workflow as part of the visual experimentation process.

Through this project, I experimented with combining 3D scanning, character transformation, and video-based storytelling as a way to explore identity through object-like representations.
